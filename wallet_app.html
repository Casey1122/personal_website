Wallet App

Managing expenses and recurring payments could be painful for modern people. Having multiple transactions, monthly bills and subscriptions to deal with, that actually sounds a lot! Fortunately, Wallet App has come to the solution. It lets users enter expenses and bills all in one place for an easy, learnable and speedy way. It’s just Aspirin for expense tracking!

Challenge

The initial idea was to prototype a comprehensive tracking app to help. There are tons of similar apps in the market, offering unlimited possibilities. However, some of these features were not supported in Hong Kong, like tracking your bank account or credit card. In such a way, the best way to do that was offering cashing and recurring tracking.

Goals

This project aims to tackle the complexity of managing personal expenses, providing a better alternative for everyone who is having problems with budgeting.

Idea Transformation

I have sketched several layouts on paper and illustrated a flow of entering expenses manually. I have done a little bit research of art direction and tried to include illustrations to style the app interface.

Idea 1: Trim down unnecessary information

It seems that third party apps can hardly provide automation. Instead of a high level of automation, recurring expenses are added to the app. It is able to solve certain bills or transactions that happen periodically.

Idea 2: Enjoyable Visual Element

There is a trend that more apps tend to adapt a cuter style or gamification to make tracking enjoyable. Some of them even provide an achievement function to encourage users’ engagement. Similarly, I tried to provide explanational illustrations and make the app less formal at the same time.

Idea 3: Privacy and Synchronization

It would be beneficial if an application could synchronize data or even export it to a spreadsheet. Definitely, levering passcode login is almost a standard.
