Wallet App

Managing expenses and recurring payments could be painful for modern people. Having multiple transactions, monthly bills and subscriptions to deal with, that actually sounds a lot! Fortunately, Wallet App has come to the solution. It lets users enter expenses and bills all in one place for an easy, learnable and speedy way. It’s just Aspirin for expense tracking!

Process

User diary to reflect area of improvement

Challenge

The initial idea was to prototype a comprehensive tracking app to help. There are tons of similar apps in the market, offering unlimited possibilities. However, some of these features were not supported in Hong Kong, like tracking your bank account or credit card. In such a way, the best way to do that was offering cashing and recurring tracking.

Goals

This project aims to tackle the complexity of managing personal expenses, providing a better alternative for everyone who is having problems with budgeting.

Idea Transformation

I have sketched several layouts and illustrated a flow of entering expenses manually. I have done a little bit research of art direction and tried to include illustrations to style the app interface.

Idea 1: Use of Recurring Entry

While full automation may not be supported, adding a recurring entry function for users might help. For certain kinds of payments, they recur after a set period of time. That could be productive to reduce complexity.

Idea 2: Enjoyable Visual Element

There is a trend that more apps tend to adapt a cuter style or gamification to make tracking enjoyable. Some of them even provide an achievement function to encourage users’ engagement. Similarly, providing illustrations could make the app less tedious.

Idea 3: Privacy and Synchronization

It would be beneficial if an application could synchronize data or even export it to a spreadsheet. Besides, using a passcode to login is definitely a standard.

A fun style is adopted to the design system. All these elements aim to provide enjoyable moments to users while tracking expenses.

Adding some pleasurable elements to the app, making the process less dull and tedious. I look for inspiration from modern silhouette graphics and color palettes which can be applied. Appealing elements give different moods to the users which makes the money tracking process pleasing.

Testing

Prototype testing - Recruiting users to perform tasks, such as adding expenses and monthly budget, also checking overall expense. Users could test out for a long period of time, say 1 week to 1 month, jot down their routine in the user diary. Designers could then review and interview users for insights that help improvement.
